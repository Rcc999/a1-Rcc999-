# Ex.5.1

The MiniPi looks unstable because this kinematic controller doesn't account for balance. A quadruped is fine with three legs on the ground but  a biped loses its support as soon as one foot lifts the ground. This causes the robot to tip over since the center of mass isn't being taken into account. Also because the IK only solves for position and not orientation the feet don't stay flat, this results as we've seen in the video in a weird "toe-down" movement instead of flat ground contact.

To fix this we would need a system that constantly shifts the robot's body weight to keep its center of mass over the foot that is currently on the ground. We also need to change and modify the IK so that we ensure that the feet stay parallel to the floor during the whole step. If we put this on real hardware the robot would just fall over because of all the issues mentionned above. The current code assumes the robot can follow any path perfectly but a real biped has to acknowledge gravity and manage the ground forces to stay upright.
